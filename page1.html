Solar Energy

Definition: Solar energy is harnessed from the Sun using photovoltaic (PV) cells or solar thermal systems. It is widely used for electricity generation, heating, and industrial applications.

Types of Solar Energy:

  • Photovoltaic (PV) Systems – Converts sunlight into electricity.
  • Solar Thermal Systems – Uses heat from the sun for water and space heating.
  • Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) – Uses mirrors to concentrate sunlight and generate steam to drive turbines.

Applications:

  • Residential: Solar panels for home electricity, solar water heaters, solar lighting.
  • Commercial & Industrial: Solar power plants, solar heating for manufacturing processes.
  • Agriculture: Solar-powered irrigation, greenhouse heating.
  • Transportation: Solar-powered vehicles, charging stations.
  • Space Exploration: Solar panels on satellites and space stations.

Advantages:

  • Renewable, sustainable, and widely available.
  • Low operating costs after installation.
  • Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.

Challenges:

  • Intermittency due to weather and nighttime.
  • Requires large space for large-scale installations.
  • High initial costs and need for energy storage solutions.
