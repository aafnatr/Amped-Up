Hydraulic (Hydropower) Energy

Definition: Hydropower generates electricity using the movement of water, typically from dams, rivers, or ocean currents. It is one of the oldest and most widely used renewable energy sources.

Types of Hydropower:

  • Conventional Dams & Reservoirs – Stores water and releases it to generate electricity.
  • Run-of-the-River Systems – Uses natural river flow without large reservoirs.
  • Pumped Storage Hydropower – Stores water in an upper reservoir and releases it when needed.
  • Tidal and Wave Energy – Harnesses ocean tides and waves for power generation.

Applications:

  • Electricity Generation: Hydropower plants supply energy to the grid.
  • Irrigation & Agriculture: Water pumping for crops and livestock.
  • Industrial Use: Provides power for mills, manufacturing, and mining.
  • Flood Control & Water Supply: Dams help manage water resources.

Advantages:

  • Reliable and produces consistent energy.
  • High energy conversion efficiency.
  • Can store energy for later use (pumped storage).

Challenges:

  • Large-scale projects disrupt ecosystems and require significant land.
  • Droughts and water shortages reduce efficiency.
  • High initial investment and long construction periods.
